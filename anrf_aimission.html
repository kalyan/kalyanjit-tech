Institution Selection Framework

Evaluating academic partners is critical. Our analysis suggests a tiered approach. Use this checklist as a starting point for your due diligence.

  • Tier-1 (IITs, IISc): High-impact, high-competition. Best for large-scale foundation model projects.
  • Tier-2/3 (NITs, IIITs): Strong domain expertise, more flexible. Ideal for applied AI and niche problem areas.
  • Faculty Assessment: Look for recent (3-5 years) publications in target AI methods and domain.
  • Lab Infrastructure: Assess existing compute, data access, and PhD student pipeline.
  • Collaboration History: Prior experience with industry is a major plus.

Value Proposition for Academia

Your offer must be compelling:

  • Real-world industry datasets.
  • Clear commercialization pathways.
  • Co-funding and access to industry-grade compute.
  • Career opportunities for students.

Key Compliance & Funding Rules

Understanding the rules is non-negotiable. Here are the critical points from our analysis of the ANRF guidelines.

  • PI Eligibility: PI MUST be full-time faculty/researcher at an Indian institution. Industry specialists act as Co-PIs or collaborators.
  • GPU & Compute Cap: Max 70% of total budget can be allocated to compute (India AI Compute Portal). This forces focus on algorithmic efficiency.
  • Open-Source Mandate: All code, models, and datasets (where feasible) MUST be released under the ANRF Open License (MIT-adapted). Plan your commercial strategy around this.
  • Data & Ethics: Approvals for human/animal data are flexible and can be obtained post-funding approval, but must be in place before work begins.

Commercialization Pathways

Given the ANRF Open-Source mandate, your business model must be built *on top* of the open deliverables. Our report identifies three primary models:

1. Open-Core Model

Release the core model (deliverable) as open-source. Sell proprietary add-ons, enterprise features (security, logging, integration), and support services.

2. SaaS & Managed Services

Host the open-source model yourself and sell access via a high-availability, managed API. You compete on reliability, performance, and ease-of-use, not the model itself.

3. Talent & IP Pipeline

Use the project to co-develop novel IP (filed jointly or by you, with non-exclusive license to ANRF). Hire the best PhDs and researchers from the project.
