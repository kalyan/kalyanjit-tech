What is APN?

The Asia-Pacific Network for Global Change Research (APN) is dedicated to fostering a dynamic community of researchers, policymakers, practitioners, and civil society. It engages in transdisciplinary approaches to build a more resilient and sustainable future by supporting research, bridging science and policy, and strengthening regional capacity.

What is the CRRP?

The Collaborative Regional Research Programme (CRRP) is APN's flagship programme. It funds regional-based research and capacity-building activities that address gaps in knowledge, contribute to policy development, and build strong collaborations across the Asia-Pacific region to tackle global change challenges.

Key Programme Information

This section highlights the core features of the CRRP, including its main objectives, the two-stage application process, and the available funding tiers. Use this information to align your project's scope and budget.

Core Features & Objectives

  • ✓ Supports regional-based collaborative research and capacity building in global change.
  • ✓ Requires collaboration among researchers from at least 3 APN member countries.
  • ✓ Encourages transdisciplinary, co-design, and co-production of knowledge with stakeholders.
  • ✓ Aims to contribute to policy-making and practical actions for sustainability.
  • ✓ Follows a two-stage application process: (1) Summary Proposal, (2) Full Proposal.

The Two-Stage Application Process

The CRRP uses a two-stage process. You must first submit a Summary Proposal (Stage 1). If successful, you will be invited to submit a Full Proposal (Stage 2). This section details the required components for each stage, with a focus on the Stage 1 Summary Proposal.

Summary Proposal Components (Appendix 2)

1. Cover Sheet (Click to expand/collapse)

Proponents must complete the cover sheet provided in the APNIS system, which includes:

  • Project Title
  • Proponent (Name, Affiliation, Contact)
  • Abstract (max 200 words)
  • Keywords (up to 5)
  • Duration of Project (1 or 2 years)
  • Total Amount Requested from APN (USD)
  • Co-funding (if any)
2. Summary of Proposal (Max 3 pages) (Click to expand/collapse)

This is the core of your Stage 1 application. It must include:

  • Background and Rationale: Why is this project needed? (max 200 words)
  • Aims/Objectives: What will the project achieve? (max 100 words)
  • Methodology/Approach: How will you conduct the research? (max 500 words)
  • Expected Outputs: What tangible products will result? (e.g., reports, papers, workshops) (max 150 words)
  • Potential Outcomes/Impacts: What changes or benefits will the project lead to? (max 150 words)
  • Relevance to APN: How does it align with APN's goals? (max 100 words)
  • Policy Relevance: How will it inform policy or decision-making? (max 100 words)
3. Proponent & Collaborators (Max 1 page) (Click to expand/collapse)

A list of the Proponent and major collaborators, including:

  • Name, Organisation, Postal Address, Email
  • Country (must be at least 3 APN member countries)
  • Briefly describe the role of each collaborator.
4. CVs (Max 2 pages) (Click to expand/collapse)
  • A 1-page CV for the Proponent (lead researcher).
  • A 1-page CV for the national focal point (if different from Proponent).
  • CVs are NOT required for other collaborators at Stage 1.

Full Proposal Components (Appendix 3)

This content is required only if you are invited to Stage 2. It builds upon the Summary Proposal.

1. Cover Sheet & Abstract (Click to expand/collapse)

An updated cover sheet and a refined abstract (max 300 words), plus a lay-person summary (max 150 words).

2. Detailed Proposal (Max 10 pages) (Click to expand/collapse)

A comprehensive document detailing:

  • Background and Rationale (expanded)
  • Aims and Objectives (detailed)
  • Methodology, Approach, and Work Plan
  • Expected Outputs and Outcomes (detailed)
  • Data Management Plan
  • Communication and Outreach Plan
  • Policy Relevance and Stakeholder Engagement
  • Capacity Building and Enhancement
  • Gender and Social Inclusion
3. Appendices (Click to expand/collapse)
  • Appendix 1: Timeline: A full month-by-month timeline.
  • Appendix 2: Budget: A detailed budget estimate in USD with year-by-year breakdown.
  • Appendix 3: Collaborators & CVs: Full list of all collaborators and CVs (2 pages for Proponent, 1 page for others).
  • Appendix 4: Logical Framework Matrix: A logframe for project management.

Mark your calendar. Here are the key deadlines for the 2025 CRRP call, leading to awards starting in October 2026. (Note: These dates are illustrative based on the call's timeline).

Notification of Stage 1 Results

Deadline for Full Proposals (Stage 2)

May 1, 2026

Proposal Review Criteria

How will your proposal be evaluated? This section breaks down the five core review criteria from Appendix 1. Use this as a checklist to strengthen your proposal and ensure it aligns with APN's priorities.

  • Is the rationale for the project clearly stated?
  • Is the project scientifically sound and the methodology appropriate?
  • Does the project address a gap in knowledge?
  • Are the aims and objectives clear, concise, and achievable?
  • Does the proposal clearly demonstrate active regional collaboration?
  • Does the project involve at least three APN member countries?
  • Does the project foster transdisciplinary, co-design, and co-production of knowledge?
  • Does the project involve appropriate stakeholders (policymakers, civil society) from the beginning?
  • Is the project relevant to policy-making processes?
  • Are the communication and outreach plans clear and effective for engaging stakeholders?
  • Does the project demonstrate a clear pathway to impact?
  • Does the project contribute to building scientific capacity in the region?
  • Does it support the development of early-career researchers?
  • Are capacity-building elements well-integrated into the project plan?
  • Is the project feasible within the proposed timeframe and budget?
  • Is the budget realistic, well-justified, and compliant with APN financial regulations?
  • Is co-funding secured or realistically planned?
