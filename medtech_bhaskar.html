Project Nexus

Fusing MAHA MedTech Mission Goals with Proven Orthopaedic R&D Expertise

Executive Summary

This report outlines 20 high-impact medical technology projects designed to be pursued under the umbrella of the MAHA MedTech Mission. The projects are strategically curated to lie at the precise intersection of the Mission's national objectives (e.g., reducing 70-80% import dependency, focusing on AI, robotics, and implants) and the specific, proven expertise of Dr. Bhaskar Borgohain.

Dr. Borgohain's profile as an academic leader, a high-volume surgeon, and a successful R&D collaborator makes him a pivotal figure for translating the Mission's vision into tangible, indigenously-developed ("Atmanirbhar") solutions. The projects are grouped into five strategic categories reflecting this synergy.

Strategic Context

MAHA MedTech Mission

Aims to dismantle India's 70-80% import dependency on medical devices. It prioritizes high-impact areas, including advanced implants, diagnostics, AI/ML, and robotics, through a mission-mode ecosystem approach.

Dr. Bhaskar Borgohain's Profile

Represents a rare blend of deep clinical expertise (orthopaedics, trauma, sports injury, joint replacement) and proven R&D capabilities. His past projects are direct precursors to the type of innovation the MAHA Mission seeks to fund.

Conclusion

The 20 projects outlined in this report represent a strategic and actionable roadmap. They are designed to be immediately pursuable by leveraging the powerful synergy between the MAHA MedTech Mission's national goals and the proven, on-the-ground R&D and clinical expertise exemplified by Dr. Bhaskar Borgohain. These projects move beyond simple ideas, providing a clear pathway to develop tangible, high-impact medical technologies that can lead India to self-reliance.
