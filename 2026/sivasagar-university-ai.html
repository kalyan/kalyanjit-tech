Essential AI Tools for Faculty

To navigate the vast ecosystem of AI, we have categorized the most relevant tools based on your daily academic workflow. Select a category below to explore specific tools, their primary functions, and practical use cases tailored for Sivasagar University's academic environment.

🤓

Consensus

An AI search engine specialized in scientific research. It extracts key findings directly from peer-reviewed papers.

Use Case: Rapidly literature mapping for a new grant proposal or verifying a specific scientific claim.
🔍

Elicit

Automates research workflows, such as parts of literature review, data extraction from PDFs, and concept synthesis.

Use Case: Uploading 10 PDFs on a topic and asking Elicit to extract the methodology and sample size from all of them into a table.
📝

ChatPDF / SciSpace

Allows you to "chat" with a specific document. Ask questions, request summaries, and find specific formulas or arguments.

Use Case: Quickly understanding the core arguments of a dense 40-page journal article before assigning it to students.
💬

ChatGPT (Plus/Edu)

The versatile foundational model. Excellent for brainstorming, drafting syllabi, and creating varied explanations for concepts.

Use Case: "Generate 3 different analogies to explain quantum entanglement to first-year undergraduate students."
📺

Gamma.app

An AI-powered presentation generator. It turns text prompts or outlines into beautifully formatted slide decks instantly.

Use Case: Converting your lecture notes document into a visual presentation draft within minutes to save formatting time.
🎨

Claude 3 (Anthropic)

Known for larger context windows and highly nuanced, less "robotic" writing compared to others. Great for document analysis.

Use Case: Uploading your entire course curriculum and asking it to identify gaps or suggest updated reading materials.
✔️

Gradescope (with AI features)

A platform for grading paper-based, digital, and code assignments. Its AI assists in grouping similar answers to grade in batches.

Use Case: Scanning 100 midterm papers; the AI groups all identical short-answer responses so you can grade them simultaneously with one click.
📊

Advanced Data Analysis (ChatGPT)

Allows uploading of CSV/Excel files. The AI can write Python code to analyze data, create charts, and find trends instantly.

Use Case: Uploading anonymized student assessment scores to identify which specific topics the class struggled with the most.

The Way Forward: Ethics & Strategy

Adopting AI is not just about using new tools; it requires a pedagogical shift. Sivasagar University must balance innovation with academic integrity. This section outlines the strategic framework and ethical boundaries faculty should observe when integrating AI into their practices. Review the core pillars below.

📜

1. Transparent Syllabi

Clearly define in your syllabus what level of AI usage is permitted for assignments. Move away from binary "banned/allowed" to specific use-case guidelines.

🧐

2. Rethink Assessment

Shift from pure recall tests to assessments that require critical thinking, in-class application, and critique of AI-generated content. "Process over Product."

🕵🏽‍☎️

3. The "Human-in-the-Loop"

Never rely on AI for final academic judgments, grading, or sensitive student communications without rigorous human review. AI hallucinates and contains biases.

👩‍🏫

4. Empowering Students

Teach students AI literacy. Guide them on how to prompt effectively, verify information, and use AI as a tutor rather than a bypass for learning.
