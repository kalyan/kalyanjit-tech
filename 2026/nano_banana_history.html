Methodology for Educators

Image generation in history is not just about "looking cool." It is a rigorous process of synthesis. We recommend a four-stage approach when using Nano Banano in curriculum.

1

Primary Source Review

Before prompting, identify text or archaeological evidence (e.g., "Herodotus describes the walls as..."). This grounds the AI in reality.

2

Iterative Prompting

Use our Prompt Lab to generate initial drafts. Adjust "Atmospheric Detail" if the lighting feels too modern or cinematic.

3

Anachronism Audit

Review the output for hallucinations. AI might add modern buttons to a 12th-century tunic. These errors are excellent "teaching moments."

4

Digital Archiving

Tag the image with the specific prompt and model used. Transparency in AI generation is critical for academic integrity.
