The Triangle Method

1. State the Problem

Start Center or slightly Left. Establish the status quo or the issue at hand. Ground yourself.

2. Tell the Story

Move closer to the audience (Front Left or Center). Lower your voice. Make it personal.

3. The Solution

Move decisively to Stage Right (the audience's right). This represents the future, the solution, and the way forward.

The Attention Architecture

You don't have the audience's attention for the full time. You must earn it repeatedly. Structure your content to match the human attention span.

The 10-Minute Rule & Attention Spikes

Insight: Attention naturally decays. Every 10 minutes (or sooner), you must insert a "Soft Break" (video, story, demo, question) to reset the clock.

The PREP Framework

Use this for impromptu speaking or short segments.

  • P
    Point: State your main idea immediately.
  • R
    Reason: Explain why this point is true/important.
  • E
    Example: Give evidence, data, or a story.
  • P
    Point: Restate the main idea.
