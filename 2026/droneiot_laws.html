2. The IoT Framework: Data & Connectivity

Drones are essentially flying IoT devices. Their regulation intersects with telecommunications standards and the new Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act.

DPDP Act 2023 Impact

  • 1 Data Fiduciary: Drone operators collecting images/video of people are 'Fiduciaries' and liable for data misuse.
  • 2 Notice & Consent: Explicit consent is required before capturing personal data, unless for specific legitimate uses (e.g., medical emergency).
  • 3 Penalty: Up to ‚Çπ250 Crore for failure to prevent data breaches.

Connectivity & Spectrum (DoT)

IoT devices in India must adhere to specific frequency allocations. The DoT has de-licensed certain bands to foster the IoT ecosystem.
