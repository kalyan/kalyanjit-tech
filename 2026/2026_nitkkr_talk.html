Foundational Corporate Structuring & Government Recognition

Before seeking investment rounds, a startup must construct a legally defensible corporate foundation. Standardizing founder equity splits, establishing vesting parameters, and securing validation from government bodies ensures long-term corporate health.

⚖

1. Optimal Entity Selection

How does a Private Limited compare to partnerships and LLPs in India?

  • ✔ Pvt Ltd: Institutional VC requirement, facilitates equity dilution, option pools (ESOPs), and transfers.
  • ✔ LLP: Good for bootstrapping but struggles to accommodate venture scale equity models.
  • ✔ Sole Proprietorship: Complete exposure to personal liability. Avoid for high-tech ventures.
* MCA SPICe+ digital registration workflow is mandatory.
👥

2. Co-Founder Agreements & Vesting

Mitigate legal splits by enforcing structured intellectual property and stock plans early.

  • ✔ IP Assignment: Ensure all proprietary code and designs are fully owned by the corporate entity.
  • ✔ Reverse Vesting: Standard 4-year vesting schedules with a mandatory 1-year cliff.
  • ✔ Bad Leaver Clauses: Define exact metrics for clawing back stock during unexpected partner exits.
* Guards the cap table from early fragmentation.
🎖

3. DPIIT Government Benefits

Unlocking India's national fiscal frameworks for validated startup entities.

  • ✔ 80-IAC Tax Holiday: 100% tax exemption on corporate profits for 3 out of the first 10 consecutive years.
  • ✔ Angel Tax Abolition (S. 56): Total release from valuation disputes over fair market value premiums.
  • ✔ Fast-Track Patents: Up to an 80% discount on filing costs plus expedited IP examination tracks.
* Startups must register via the Startup India portal.

Navigating Section 3(k) of the Indian Patents Act

Section 3(k) explicitly excludes "a mathematical or business method or a computer programme per se or algorithms" from patent protection to keep pure abstractions in the public domain. To successfully protect your Deeptech, AI/ML, or Web3 blockchain IP, your patent claims must reflect a demonstrable hardware interaction.

The DPDP Act 2023 Compliance Blueprint

Under India's Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023, startups collecting user information are designated as "Data Fiduciaries" and face statutory penalties of up to INR 250 crore for processing failures or breaches.

Offshore Holding Companies & Outbound Regulations

For startups seeking global VCs, establishing an offshore holding company (e.g., in Singapore or Delaware) is a highly strategic milestone, commonly known as "flipping". This process is governed under FEMA's Overseas Investment (OI) Rules of 2022 and strictly monitored to prevent unauthorized "round-tripping".

Gradual Migration Model

Incorporating a new offshore entity that creates a new Indian operational subsidiary. Avoids high capital gains taxes upon direct share-swapping.

The LRS Limit

Individual Indian residents are capped at remitting a maximum of USD 250,000 per financial year for overseas direct investments.

Round-Tripping Block

Overseas investments must not create multi-tier downstream Indian subsidiaries beyond the regulatory 2-layer limit.
