Adapting the Narrative:
Climate Resilience in North East India

A strategic framework for a 12-part short film series designed to educate, engage, and mobilize the youth of the "Seven Sisters" + Sikkim through culturally relevant storytelling.

Proposed by Rahul Mahanta • Kalyanjit Hatibaruah

Why This Region? Why Now?

North East India is a biodiversity hotspot, but it is disproportionately vulnerable to climate change. From the melting glaciers of Arunachal to the flooding plains of Assam, the youth face a future defined by adaptation. This visualization maps the specific climate risks per state that drive our content strategy.

üî• The "Heat & Water" Paradox

While Assam battles annual floods, Meghalaya (the wettest place on earth) faces dry spells. The series addresses these contradictions to teach Adaptation over simple mitigation.

üåø Biodiversity Loss

Arunachal and Nagaland are seeing shifts in flora due to warming. The films will focus on Preservation of Indigenous Knowledge as a climate shield.

üèôÔ∏è Unplanned Urbanization

Landslides in Mizoram and Sikkim are exacerbated by construction. We target urban youth with messages of Sustainable Development.

Measurable Impact Strategy

We move beyond "awareness" to "behavioral change." Using the Knowledge-Attitude-Practice (KAP) model, we track how viewership translates to on-ground action in North East India.

1

Engage

Localized content in 8+ dialects delivered via WhatsApp & YouTube Shorts.

3

Students form "Eco-Squads" to audit school waste & energy use.

4

Impact

Reduction in local burning, increased tree cover, and policy petitions.
